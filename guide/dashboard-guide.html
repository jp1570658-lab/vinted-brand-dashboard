Vinted Brand Dashboard

Your simple guide to tracking bags, sales, and profit — all in one place.

EASY USER GUIDE

This dashboard helps you run your secondhand luxury bag business without spreadsheets. You add a bag, the dashboard follows it from the moment you buy it until it sells, and it shows you how much money you made. This guide explains everything in plain words.

1. What is this dashboard?

Think of it as a smart notebook for your bag business. Every bag you buy becomes a "card." Each card moves through four simple stages, so you always know where every bag is and what it's worth.

The four stages every bag goes through

StageWhat it means
Future StockYou bought the bag. It hasn't shipped to you yet.
In TransitThe bag is on its way to you.
In StockThe bag arrived and is ready to list and sell.
SoldThe bag sold. The dashboard works out your profit.

A bag always moves forward in this order. You move it with one tap when something happens.

2. What can it do? (Main features)

📊 Dashboard (home screen)

Shows your key numbers at a glance: money earned this month and this year, your profit, the value of bags you still hold, and how many bags are in each stage.

➕ Quick Intake (the gold + button)

Add a new bag in seconds. Take a photo, type the brand and model, pick the price and who sourced it, and tap save. The bag appears instantly in Future Stock.

🏷️ Inventory views

Separate screens for Future Stock, In Transit, and In Stock show your bags as picture cards. The Sold screen is a table with sale price, profit, and margin for each bag.

⏰ Aging alerts

If a bag has been sitting abroad for more than 14 days, its card turns red and pulses, and a warning bar appears at the top. This tells you which bags are tying up your money for too long.

📧 Automatic sale detection (Gmail)

When connected, it reads your Vinted "You've sold!" emails and marks the matching bag as Sold for you — no manual typing.

🧾 Expense tracking (Wise)

When connected, it pulls in your payments (shipping, runner payments, stock purchases) and sorts them into categories automatically. You can link any payment to a specific bag.

🤖 AI insights (Claude)

The dashboard can suggest a selling price and how fast a bag will sell, rank which sources (runners) make you the most money, and forecast next month's revenue.

⚙️ Settings

Manage the people who source your bags ("runners"), trigger a sync by hand, and see how the integrations are doing.

3. Getting started in 5 easy steps

  1. Open the app and log in. Type your password (in demo mode it is demo1234).
  2. Look at the Dashboard. The cards at the top show your money and how many bags you have.
  3. Add a bag. Tap the gold + button at the bottom-right, fill in the details, and save.
  4. Move the bag along. Tap a bag's card, then tap the gold button to send it to the next stage (Future Stock → In Transit → In Stock → Sold).
  5. Mark it sold. When it sells, enter the sale price. The dashboard shows your profit automatically.

4. How to get the best out of it

Add bags the moment you buy them. The sooner a bag is in the system, the more accurate your numbers and the aging timer will be.
Always add a photo. Picture cards make it much faster to recognise a bag at a glance, especially on your phone.
Watch the red cards. A red, pulsing card means a bag has been stuck for over 14 days. Chase it up — that's money sitting still.
Keep your runners up to date. When you record who sourced each bag, the AI can tell you which sources earn you the most, so you can buy smarter.
Use one currency confidently. Enter the price in the currency you actually paid (EUR, GBP, NGN, KES). The dashboard converts everything to euros for you, so profit is always comparable.
Check the Dashboard daily. Thirty seconds each morning tells you what sold, what's aging, and what to focus on.

5. Using it on your phone (accessibility)

  • Built for phones first. The layout fits small screens — no pinching or side-scrolling.
  • Big tap targets. Buttons and cards are large and easy to press with a thumb.
  • Quick Intake in a few taps. Grade and currency are tap-buttons, not fiddly menus.
  • Clear colours. Each stage has its own colour so you can read your inventory at a glance.
  • Camera friendly. Adding a photo opens your camera or photo library directly.
  • Works on computer too. On a wide screen it shows more bags side by side automatically.

7. Quick answers

How do I add a bag?

Tap the gold + button (bottom-right), fill in the details, tap save.

How do I move a bag to the next stage?

Tap the bag's card, then tap the gold "→ next stage" button in the pop-up.

Where do I see my profit?

On the Sold screen (per bag) and on the Dashboard (totals for the month and year).

What does a red bag mean?

It has been waiting more than 14 days. Follow it up — it's tying up your cash.

Do I have to type sales manually?

In Live mode with Gmail connected, no — Vinted sale emails mark bags sold for you. You can always do it by hand too.
